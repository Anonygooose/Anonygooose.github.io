About Me

Hi, I'm Goose! I am a Game Design & Development student at the Rochester Institute of Technology.
I've been interested in video games my entire life, coming up with ideas ever since I was a child.
I am interested in both the technical and design aspects of games, wanting to develop my skills in both.

I enjoy drawing, reading comics, and designing in my free time!

Skills

Coding-wise I know & understand C#, CSS and HTML, and am familiar with Visual Studio, Visual Studio Code,
Unity, and Godot. I am new to everything programming, however I have picked these up with ease.
I am always open to learning up new languages and experimenting with what I am given.

Art-wise I am comfortable with creating both realistic and cartoony art pieces, and am more than willing to
experiment with my style. I'm familiar with programs such as Photoshop, Clip Studio Paint, and Toon Boom.
I hope to progress my animation, character design & world building capabilities throughout the next few years.

Class Projects

The Eternal Crown

Artist, programmer and debugger.
A simple RPG created with a small group of people in my Game Dev & Algorithmic Problem Solving class.
The game was hardcoded in C# using MonoGame. I focused primarily on debugging, fixing & cleaning code,
as well as creating all the assets and figuring out how to implement them into the game.

You play as an adventurer whose goal is to travel through a forest in order to find "The Eternal Crown."
This crown is promised to give eternal life, and it's your goal to obtain it by whatever means necessary.

An example video of how the game works. The player travels through a maze and fights off skeletons in a turn-based combat in order to reach the end.

A simple poster created to show off the game.

Second slide of an intro-sequence. Reads: Legend says that any who wears the crown is granted the gift of Eternal Life! Many have tried to claim it, but none have prevailed."

An example end-screen. Shows kills, how much damage was dealt, how much damage was taken and the player's final score.

Dinosaur Cowboy VS The IRS

Solo project.
This was my absolute first Unity project, being tasked with creating a SHMUP without using Unity's
collision system. It was a fun task trying to figure out how to work with Unity's interface, as well as
creating a fun game.

You're a dinosaur simply living life as a cowboy. You're as happy as you can be... until you recieve a letter.
It's from the IRS!? This is outrageous, cowboys should not have to pay taxes.

An example video of how the game works. The player attacks enemies whose spawn rate increases over a period of time.
